ENS GOVERNANCE TOKEN - AIRDROP ELIGIBILITY (Oct 2021 snapshot)

The ENS airdrop distributed the ENS governance token to accounts that owned at least one .eth name during the snapshot window. Snapshot date: October 31, 2021 00:00 UTC.

A wallet was eligible for ENS if it satisfied ALL of C1, C2, and C3, with adjustments per C4 through C6.

C1. Ownership
The address owned at least one .eth name at any point in the snapshot window (May 4, 2019 through October 31, 2021 00:00 UTC).

C2. Reverse Record
The address had set a primary ENS name (also called the reverse record) at the snapshot block. Setting the reverse record was necessary to demonstrate that a human user, not a resale bot, controlled the wallet.

C3. Non-expired at snapshot
The name owned at snapshot must have been non-expired for at least a portion of the snapshot window. Names that expired before the snapshot window started did not qualify; names that expired mid-window did qualify for the pre-expiry period.

Multipliers:
C4. Length of ownership. Points scaled with total time the address held any .eth name.
C5. Registration age. Extra points if the wallet's oldest name was registered before the price adjustment on May 4, 2019.
C6. Human check. Wallets that opted in to signed messages on the Ethereum Attestation Service before Oct 31, 2021 could receive a small additional grant. This was optional and had no effect on base eligibility.

Explicit ineligibility (a wallet does not qualify regardless of C1-C6 when):
X1. The wallet's only names were reserved or premium names purchased without a reverse record set.
X2. The wallet is a known contract that automated bulk .eth registration without setting reverse records (Sybil registration bots).
X3. The wallet is a duplicate of a wallet already counted under the C1 criterion (i.e., attempted double claims).

Wallets ineligible under X1-X3 receive zero ENS. All other qualifying wallets receive base + multiplier ENS per the published grant schedule.

Source: True Names Ltd (ENS Labs) announcement, November 2021.
